What Is a Kölsch Beer?

Why is a Kölsch special?

Kölsch has an exceptional balance of malt and fruit flavors.
Beers are called either ale or lager depending on the type of yeast used in fermentation. Ales are brewed with top-fermenting yeast at higher temperatures,
which results in stronger and fruitier beers. Lager is different. Bottom-fermenting yeast is brewed at lower temperatures.
The result is fresher, more malty beers. Kölsch combines both brewing techniques.
Kölsch is therefore light and drinkable, has a hint of fruitiness and spice like ale, but has a fresher, cleaner finish.
This is why Kölsch is extremely drinkable and you should order the next one as soon as the first one is served.
Particularly well-known Kösch beers are Gaffel-Kölsch, Reissdorf-Kölsch and Früh-Klösch. Click the button to visit the Früh-Klösch brewery.
